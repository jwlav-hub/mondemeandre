人声鼎沸 — The Sound of a Room That's Alive

The Chinese have a phrase for it: 人声鼎沸 (rén shēng dǐng fèi). Human voices boiling in a cauldron. Not background music, not kitchen clatter — the particular roar that fills a room when every table is taken and no one wants to be anywhere else.

You know the sound. You've walked past a place, heard it through the glass, and turned around.

That noise is a signal. The in-crowd found this place. The locals came back. The room filled up before the reviews caught up. 人声鼎沸 doesn't happen by accident — it happens when the energy is right, the menu earns it, and the city has quietly decided.
