### The three signature indices (draft — confirm/adjust on the ground)

**1. S-Curve Position → Take-off / inflection (steep climb).** GDP per capita PPP ~$17.5k, services-and-industry now ~80% of GDP, urbanization crossing ~40%, median age ~34, growth running 8%. Textbook inflection — industrializing fast, urbanizing, demographically still young. *Your read: cranes and scaffolding count; motorbikes vs. cars; first malls and coffee chains.*

**2. Tourist–Local Spread → provisional 1.3–2× (moderate two-tier).** Can't score from the desk — this is your street pass. Cost base is very low (pho ~$1.50, draft beer often <$1, fiber ~$11/mo, prepaid data ~$3/mo). *Your read: same bottle backstreet vs. tourist strip in District 1; metered vs. quoted taxi; Mui Ne resort strip vs. fishing-village prices.*

**3. Localization Factor → Mixed, with a twist.** Export economy is globalized and FDI-driven (Samsung/LG alone ~30% of exports; FDI ~9–10% of GDP). But the domestic high street is strikingly local — Vietnamese coffee chains (Highlands, Trung Nguyen, Phúc Long), local F&B, and a large cash informal sector. The blunt test ("could you spend a week without paying a multinational?") probably passes more easily here than the FDI headline suggests. *Your read: walk a District 1 commercial street and tally local vs. global storefronts; local roaster vs. Starbucks; local hotels vs. Marriott/Accor.*

## The "next Singapore" framing — what the data says

The thesis you heard on the road has real support and one big asterisk.

**Support:** 8% growth, the strongest since 2011; an electronics export base that crossed **$107bn** in 2025 (≈23% of all exports); FDI disbursement at a five-year high ($27.6bn); top-20 global internet speeds (fixed broadband ~262 Mbps, 10th–13th worldwide); 87% banked with QR payments up ~160% in value YoY; public debt low (~33% of GDP) and an IMF verdict of "low risk of sovereign distress." This is a country visibly *becoming*.

**The asterisk — three things the bull case glosses:**
- **The 20% US tariff (and 40% transshipment levy).** Vietnam's model *is* export manufacturing to the US ($123.5bn two-way in 2024). The Oct-2025 framework locks in 20% — manageable but a margin tax on the core engine, and the 40% transshipment rule is aimed squarely at "China-plus-one" rerouting, which is a chunk of the FDI story.
- **Thin reserves.** ~$83.6bn against a ~$465bn import bill is only **~2.2 months of cover** — under the 3-month adequacy line, and the IMF flagged it "moderately below adequacy." The SBV has been intervening to keep the dong from sliding. Singapore this is not — yet.
- **Imported energy + inflation.** Vietnam is a net importer of crude, refined fuel, and coal. The May-2026 CPI spike to 5.6% (highest since Jan 2020) was driven by transport/energy off the Middle East conflict. A young export economy priced in dollars that imports its energy takes an FX shock and a fuel shock together.

## Full dossier by section

### A. Currency & FX
- **USD/VND ~26,315** (30 Jun 2026); 30-day range 26,289–26,345. Down ~0.7% over 12 months — a slow managed depreciation, not a crisis slide. *(Trading Economics)*
- SBV intervened through 2024–25 to contain FX volatility; the dong is tightly managed against the dollar. *Street check: bank vs. airport vs. gold-shop rates; do locals save/price big items in USD or gold?*

### B. Prices, Inflation & Purchasing Power
- **CPI +5.60% YoY (May 2026)**, highest since Jan 2020; core +4.67%. Monthly pace cooling (+0.29%). Transport +12.5% YoY the main driver (energy). *(Trading Economics / NSO)*
- Govt holding its **4.5% inflation target** for 2026 despite the energy shock.
- **Policy rate 4.5%** (refinancing), 3.0% discount — unchanged since Jun 2023; accommodative to chase the 10% growth ambition. *(SBV)*
- **GDP per capita:** nominal ~$5,026 (2025) → ~$5,115 (2026e); **PPP ~$16,386 (2024)**, ~$17,500 (2026e). The nominal-vs-PPP gap (~3.4×) is the bargain you feel on arrival. *(IMF WEO / World Bank / NSO)*
- Cost-of-living anchors (Jun 2026, HCMC): pho ~$1.50, banh mi $0.60–1, three meals at stalls $4–6/day, prepaid unlimited data ~$3/mo, fiber ~$11/mo, 1-BR city-center rent ~£408/mo. Avg wage Q4-2025 ~$331/mo. *(Numbeo / International Living)*

### C. Growth & Economic Structure
- **Real GDP +8.02% (2025)** — best since 2011; **Q1-2026 +7.83%** (industry/construction +8.9%, services +8.2%, agri +3.6%). Govt targets ~10% for 2026; banks/IMF more like 6.5–7.5%. *(NSO / World Bank)*
- **Sector shares (2025):** agriculture 11.6%, industry & construction 37.7%, services 42.8% (+ taxes 8.0%). Industry contributed ~46% of growth, services ~49%. *(NSO)*
- Employment: agriculture still ~25% of jobs but falling; services/industry pay ~2× agriculture.
- **Urbanization 40.2% (2024)**, govt targeting 45%+ then 50% by 2030 — the S-curve in motion.
- **Informal economy ~21.5% of GDP** (shadow-economy estimate); informal *employment* far higher — much of the street is cash and unregistered. *(World Economics)*

### D. External Position & Vulnerability
- **Current account ~+4% of GDP (2025e)**, surplus narrowing from ~5.9% (2024) as export growth cools. *(IMF Art. IV)*
- **FX reserves ~$83.6bn (Dec 2025)** ≈ **~2.2 months import cover** — below the 3-month tripwire; IMF: "moderately below adequacy." **Key vulnerability.**
- **Public debt ~33% of GDP** (PPG 31.3%); fiscal deficit ~1.5%; IMF "low risk of sovereign distress." Solvency is a genuine strength. *(IMF)*
- **FDI 2025:** $38.4bn registered, $27.6bn disbursed (5-yr high). Sources: **Singapore $4.8bn (28%), China $3.6bn (21%)**, Hong Kong, Japan $1.6bn, Sweden, Korea. China rising, Korea/HK falling. *Street check: which flags on the new towers and industrial parks?* *(MPI / VnEconomy)*
- **Energy: net importer** of crude (14.1Mt), refined fuel (9.9Mt, ~$6.8bn), and coal (67Mt in 2024). Electricity ~48% coal, ~46% low-carbon (hydro ~⅓). A USD-priced energy import bill = twin FX/inflation exposure. *(EIA / IEA)*
- **Trade composition:** total turnover **$930bn (2025)**. Exports led by electronics (~35%; computers/components $107.75bn alone), textiles & footwear (18%), machinery (10%), agriculture (9%), wood/furniture (7%). Heavy reliance on Chinese/Korean upstream inputs. Top destinations: US, EU, China. *(NSO / Vietnam News)*
- **US tariff:** 20% reciprocal on Vietnamese goods (from Jul 2025), **40% on transshipped goods**; framework agreement 26 Oct 2025; VN opening market to US goods in return. The single biggest macro swing factor. *(USTR / White House)*
- **IMF status: no lending program** — Article IV surveillance only (concluded Sep 2025). Healthy.

### E. Stability, Governance & Safety
- **Corruption (CPI 2025): 41/100, rank ~81/180** — improving trend since 2012 ("blazing furnace" anti-graft drive); petty corruption better managed than peers. *(Transparency Intl)*
- **Global Peace Index 2025: 38th of 163** — above South Korea, Indonesia, much of the region. One-party state = political stability priced low. *(IEP)*
- *Street check: police interactions, bribe expectations, mood; night-walk safety; common scams (taxi, the bill).*

### F. Infrastructure
- **Internet: top-20 globally on both fronts** — fixed broadband ~262 Mbps (10th–13th), mobile ~102 Mbps (13th–19th), 5G ~429 Mbps. Viettel ranked #3 fastest mobile network globally. Cheap and fast. *(Ookla)*
- **Banking/payments: ~87% of adults banked**; ~10.4m mobile-money accounts (⅓ rural); QR payments +160% in value YoY (H1-2025). Tap/QR is everywhere in cities. *(SBV / Findex 2025)*
- *Street check: airport arrival impression; the ride in (roads/potholes); usable public transit without a local (HCMC Metro Line 1 now open); power flickers / generators; coverage & real speed on your SIM.*

### G. Health & Disease Risk
- **Dengue endemic, year-round, peaks in rainy season**; >170,000 cases in 2024, 2025 similar — Vietnam among countries reporting elevated counts. Primary traveler health risk. *(CDC)*
- **Malaria: low/negligible in cities** (none in HCMC, Hanoi, Da Nang, Nha Trang); chemoprophylaxis only for rural non-delta areas. *(CDC Yellow Book)*
- *Street check: mosquito load in Mui Ne/Saigon; pharmacy & clinic visibility; what locals do about bites.*

### H. Distribution & Society
- **Gini ~36 (2022, latest)** — moderate, *lower* than Malaysia/Philippines (both ~41) and improving. The dual economy is real but not extreme by regional standards. *(World Bank / OECD)*
- **Demographics:** median age ~34; 67.6% working-age, 22.9% under 15, 9.5% over 65; dependency ratio 47.8% (low). Population ~101.6m (15th largest). Demographic dividend still open but the window is closing fast (aging ~4.3 yrs/decade). *(UN / Worldometer)*
- **Tourism: record 21.2m international arrivals (2025), +20.4% YoY**, above pre-COVID; govt targeting tourism at 13–14% of GDP by 2030. China/Japan/India/Russia leading the inbound surge. *(Vietnam National Authority of Tourism)*
