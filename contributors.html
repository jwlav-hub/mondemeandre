Contributors

Travel Further Together

Monde Méandre is built around the idea that the best travel insight comes from people who have actually been there — who ate the meal, walked the street, made the wrong turn that turned into the right one. If that's you, we'd like to hear from you.

Your Journey. Our Platform.

We're open to contributors who want to share their experiences with a community of culturally curious travelers. Not travel influencers — travelers. People who go somewhere to understand it, not to photograph it for an algorithm.

What We're Looking For

The Kind of Writing That Belongs Here

✍️

Cultural Depth

Posts that go beyond logistics. We want the story behind the temple, the context around the meal, the thing you understood about a place after your second or third visit.

📷

Original Photography

Your own images from your own trips. Phone or camera, polished or raw — as long as they're honest. We're not looking for stock photo aesthetics.

🗺️

Practical Usefulness

Readers should leave knowing something they didn't before — a neighborhood worth walking, a restaurant worth planning around, a logistical nuance that saves them an afternoon.

🌏

Geographic Focus

Our core is Asia — East, Southeast, and South. We're expanding. If you've spent real time somewhere and have something genuine to say about it, pitch it.

🍜

Food & Dining

Food writing that goes beyond the review. The market stall that's been there for forty years, the dish that required pointing at something unpronounceable, the chef whose story is worth telling.

🧭

Honest Perspective

No listicles, no sponsored roundups, no "Top 10 Hidden Gems." We want a point of view and the willingness to be wrong sometimes. Uncertainty is not a weakness in travel writing.

The Travelers Behind the Blog

Joseph Laviolette

Founder & Travel Writer

Living in Macau, SAR PR China from 2010 to 2020 gave me a rare gateway to Asia, where a short flight could lead to an entirely different landscape, culture, or way of life. Monde Méandre is where I share those experiences — a travel and photo blog dedicated to the places, people, and cultures that make this part of the world so endlessly fascinating.
