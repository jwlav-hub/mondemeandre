精致穷 — The Art of Pure Indulgence with Next to Nothing

The Chinese have a phrase for it: 精致穷 (jīng zhì qióng). Exquisitely poor. The refined art of living beautifully without the budget to match.

It's a Gen Z concept — the person who saves all week to sit at the right café, who knows exactly which experiences are worth the splurge and which ones aren't. Not poverty. Curation.

Michelin figured this out long before the algorithm did. Hidden inside their guide — past the tasting menus and the expense accounts — is a category built entirely around this idea. Restaurants where the kitchen is serious, the ingredients are exceptional, and lunch costs less than a cocktail at the hotel bar.

You don't need to be wealthy to eat extraordinarily well. You need to know where to look. Enter a city below — the AI will return Michelin-recognized restaurants at budget price points: Bib Gourmand picks and affordable starred restaurants.
